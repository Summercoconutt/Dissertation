EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This report summarises an exploratory validation pipeline applied to a cross-sectional
DAO-level feature table (governance participation, voting-power structure, and
representation-normalised vote-mix variables). After cleaning, 434 DAOs enter the main
analysis; 57 DAOs (13.1%) are flagged as multivariate outliers and a parallel analysis
uses 377 DAOs without those rows.

K-means over k = 2,…,6 on robust-scaled features selects k = 2 for the full sample
(maximum silhouette coefficient ≈ 0.23). This value indicates weak-to-moderate geometric
separation of clusters in the scaled feature space. A column-permutation null benchmark
shows that, at k = 2, the mean silhouette on permuted data is higher than on the real
data; this must be interpreted carefully and precludes strong claims of “natural kinds”
or crisp typologies based on silhouette alone.

Despite modest separation metrics, the two clusters show a coherent substantive contrast:
one group combines lower unique-voter scale, lower voting-power inequality (lower Gini),
higher proposal frequency, and more conflictual voting patterns (higher against/abstain
z-scores, lower alignment with the majority); the larger group combines larger scale,
higher concentration of voting power, lower proposal frequency, and more “mainstream”
vote-mix behaviour. Results are stable across random initialisations (pairwise ARI = 1
for the stability check at the chosen k).

The recommended narrative for the dissertation is **exploratory / descriptive**:
continuous heterogeneity and outlier influence, with a **tentative** two-group partition
used for illustration—not as proof of discrete latent classes.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. RESEARCH QUESTION AND SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Question.** Do DAOs, described by governance and participation features, fall into
meaningful discrete clusters, or do they mainly vary along continuous dimensions with
a minority of extreme observations?

**Scope.** Unsupervised structure diagnostics: distributions, correlations, PCA,
partition-based clustering (K-means, hierarchical Ward), density clustering (DBSCAN;
HDBSCAN optional if installed), null benchmarking, and stability checks. Two parallel
tracks: (i) all cleaned DAOs; (ii) outliers removed.

**Non-goals.** This stage does not estimate causal effects, predict outcomes, or
optimise a production classifier. It supports thesis claims about **whether** and **how**
strongly cluster-like structure appears in the feature space.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. DATA AND PREPROCESSING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Identifier.** `space` (DAO identifier).

**Feature set (12 variables, after engineering).**  
Includes `log_n_unique_voters`, robust participation moments, Gini of voting power,
whale share (top 1%), proposal frequency (log1p where configured), repeat voter rate,
and z-scored representation metrics for vote shares (for / against / abstain), choice
entropy, and alignment with the majority.

**Cleaning.** Rows with missing values on any selected feature are dropped (2 rows lost
in the current run: 436 → 434). Features are robust-scaled for distance-based methods.

**Outliers.** A combined rule flags a DAO if any feature has |z| > 3 versus the sample
or if at least two features fall outside the 1.5×IQR fences. This yields 57 outliers
for sensitivity analysis.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. METHODS (HIGH LEVEL)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• **K-means:** k = 2…6, multiple initialisations, silhouette, Calinski–Harabasz,
  Davies–Bouldin. Best k by maximum silhouette on the full sample (k = 2).

• **Hierarchical clustering:** Ward linkage; dendrogram; metrics by k; cross-tabulation
  against K-means at the same k for agreement.

• **DBSCAN:** grid over eps and min_samples (reported in `dbscan_results*.csv`).

• **PCA:** up to 10 components; cumulative variance, loadings, eigenvector bar plots
  (PC1–PC3), scatter plots.

• **Null benchmark:** Independent column-wise permutations destroy cross-feature
  dependence; K-means silhouette is compared to the mean over permutations.

• **Stability:** K-means with multiple random seeds at fixed k; pairwise Adjusted Rand
  Index between label vectors.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. RESULTS — UNIVARIATE AND CORRELATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Skewness.** Several variables show heavy tails (e.g. abstain-related and some
representation z-scores), consistent with non-Gaussian, continuous variation rather
than well-separated spherical groups.

**High correlations.** With threshold |r| > 0.8, no pairs are flagged in
`high_corr_pairs.csv`; multicollinearity at the extreme end is limited, though moderate
correlations may still appear on the heatmap.

**Figures.** Histograms with KDE per feature (`feature_distributions/`); correlation
heatmap (`correlation_heatmap.png`).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. RESULTS — PCA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Approximate variance shares (full sample): PC1 ≈ 23.2%, PC2 ≈ 18.4%, PC3 ≈ 15.4%;
cumulative for first three components ≈ **57.0%**. No single axis dominates; 2D plots
omit substantial variance.

Loadings (interpretive, not latent constructs) emphasise combinations of vote-mix
variables and power/participation variables; eigenvector bar charts (`pca_eigenvector_PC1/2/3`)
support narrative about which features align with each major variance direction.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. RESULTS — K-MEANS (FULL SAMPLE, k = 2)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Cluster sizes.** Cluster 0: n = 138 (31.8%); Cluster 1: n = 296 (68.2%).

**Silhouette.** ≈ 0.230 (weak-to-moderate). Other k values yield lower silhouette on
this sample except local variation at k = 6 (still modest).

**Substantive profile (cluster means on original-scale features).**

*Cluster 0 (smaller group).*  
• Lower `log_n_unique_voters` (≈ 6.72 vs 7.12): smaller unique voter base on average.  
• Lower `gini_voting_power` (≈ 0.45 vs 0.65): **more equal** distribution of voting power.  
• Higher `proposal_frequency_per_30d` (≈ 2.04 vs 1.42): **more frequent governance proposals**.  
• Representation z-scores: strongly negative `z_rep_pct_for_votes` (≈ −0.80 vs −0.06);
  positive `z_rep_pct_against_votes` and `z_rep_pct_abstain_votes`; negative
  `z_rep_pct_aligned_with_majority` (≈ −0.48 vs +0.20).  
**Interpretation (exploratory):** a profile of **higher agenda intensity** and
**more conflictual or fragmented voting** relative to the sample—fewer “for” votes,
more against/abstain, and **less voting with the majority**.

*Cluster 1 (larger group).*  
• Larger voter base, **higher Gini** (more concentrated voting power).  
• Lower proposal frequency.  
• Vote-mix z-scores closer to zero and **positive alignment with the majority**.  
**Interpretation (exploratory):** a **larger-scale, more whale-skewed power structure**
with **quieter agendas** and **more “mainstream”** vote patterns in z-score terms.

These labels are **descriptive** summaries for discussion, not validated typology names.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. RESULTS — NULL BENCHMARK AND STABILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Null (column permutation).** At k = 2, mean silhouette on permuted data (≈ 0.293)
exceeds the real silhouette (≈ 0.230). This does **not** mean the clustering is
“wrong,” but it shows that **silhouette under this null design is not higher on the
real joint structure than on column-shuffled data** at k = 2. Discussion points:
limitations of the null (marginals preserved), class imbalance effects on silhouette,
and the need for multiple evidence lines (visual, hierarchical agreement, substantive
readability).

**Stability.** Pairwise ARI across seeds equals 1.0 in the saved summary: the **same
partition** is recovered—high **reproducibility** of the algorithm given fixed k and
data. This does not imply strong “natural” separation; it implies **deterministic
labelling of the same optimum** under the experimental settings.

10. LIMITATIONS (SUITABLE TO STATE IN THE MEETING)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. **Exploratory clustering** — no hold-out validation; metrics are internal.  
2. **Silhouette** is sensitive to cluster shape, balance, and scale; weak values caution
   against over-interpretation.  
3. **Null benchmark** is one of many possible nulls; results are **design-dependent**.  
4. **Cross-section** — dynamic DAO behaviour is not modelled.  
5. **Feature selection** drives results; sensitivity to alternative feature sets should
   be acknowledged.
